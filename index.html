About Me

I'd like to parlay my skills in design and creating into an exciting career in UX. I studied Graphic Design and Product Design at Pennsylvania State University. I have since used those skills as a: freelance designer for various recording artists, small business owner, and bicycle builder. UX, for me, is a tremendous form of expression in engaging the audience with a thoughtful discussion. It allows me to tinker and manipulate my work-- it allows me to surprise myself with new and interessting concepts that arrive at near-perfection. UX should be clear, concise, and easily read.



I have been volunteering at Neighborhood Bike Works for a decade, an avid, award-winning homebrewer for 12 years with recipes ranging from SMASH (single malt & single hops) to better understand my ingredients to experimentations with unique, unnavigated territories such as a Dandelion Saison and a back-sweetened blueberry wheat beer. I am a Guinness World Record holder: longest distance to shoot a pumpkin (5545 ft). Have been building computers for 6 years. I'm an athlete and outdoorsman (completed through-hike of the Appalachian Trail from Maine to Georgia in 2008, ran two marathons with a PR of 3:51:45), and lastly, I'm an Eagle Scout.

Marshall's Market Logo

Hinterhaus Album

I wanted to tease the concept of the hinterhaus (german: meaning house behind the backyard of a house adjoining the street) into a visual medium. The effect was a simple, ornamental exterior and a muted, exposed interior.

Animanatomy Album

This quirky group out of Pittsburgh and surrounding coal towns needed a playful album cover. Brad, the bassists for the band, was inspired by one of his anatomy texts for how the cover should read. I acted as a vessel to bring the image in his head to 2D object.
